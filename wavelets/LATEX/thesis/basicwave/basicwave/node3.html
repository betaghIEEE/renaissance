Wavelets via Convolution

Wavelets are defined in terms of average and difference components. Each component can further be transformed to isolate properties of each component. Typically, each component has the form where O is the original signal, A is the average component, D is the difference component and the signal A concatenated with D. Each of these components are produced by an orthonormal basis. Also, these components are produced such that the original can be constructed easily from them.


Many mathematicians such as Walker[2], use a form that eliminates half of the values. Thus a form can be defined which has the same number of elements as the original. The rules for choosing the member elements are dependent on the wavelet filter choice.
